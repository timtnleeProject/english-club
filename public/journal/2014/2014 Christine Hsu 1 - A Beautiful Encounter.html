May 31, 2014

A Beautiful Encounter

“I will go to Taiwan in early April.” When I got Maureen’s mail where she said that she and her husband would visit Taiwan again, I felt extremely excited. Immediately I asked her to spare some time for me during her stay in Taipei.

If I did not finish a part-time job in Oct. 2012, I would not have got the opportunity to work as a clerk at Judy Ongg’s wooden print exhibition which was held at National Dr. Sun Yat-Sen Memorial Hall. If Maureen and her husband did not come to see her daughter, Ellisa, who was a teacher at Taipei European School, they would not have visited National Dr. Sun Yat-Sen and have met me there. They arrived on a Saturday morning when there were not many guests so we chatted a lot. Knowing that she was an Australian as well as a volunteer worker for breast cancer patients, I could not wait to share with her my travel experiences to Melbourne and what I had done with Taiwan breast cancer survivors. Besides taking pictures, we also exchanged contact information.

If we had not had that beautiful encounter two years ago, I would not have got the chance to meet Maureen’s daughter and her two good friends last month. The day before they left Taiwan, we had hot pot for dinner. They carefully followed my instructions to cook every dish step by step, and greatly enjoyed the rare first-time experience. Afterwards, I took them to Qing-Kuang Market, where we had a leisurely stroll in the cool night.

Maureen and her two friends are all retired from the same school and they traveled together very often, but if Ellisa had not taught in Taipei, her two friends would never have thought to come here. They felt lucky to have both Ellisa and me to be their tour guides. It was indeed a fantastic night and I had to thank all my fellow students in the English Club. If we had not studied English together for such a long time, I would not have made friends with foreigners and explored cultures around the world.
