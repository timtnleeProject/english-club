Nov. 26, 2010

In House

“Only spending NT$150, you can have an enjoyable afternoon tea at a music restaurant, where you can taste unlimited cakes and cookies.” I got the review of the restaurant, In House, from one of my friends, but I wondered if it really cost NT$150 only.

Last month, I got a chance to explore this restaurant. From Google maps, I found it was very close to the Vieshow Cinema. The restaurant was hidden behind a small garden. Peering through the garden, I saw its inside decoration was exactly the same as shown in the pictures I searched from the Internet. Stepping into the restaurant and seeing the comfortable environment, I really doubted if it would cost only NT$150 for an afternoon tea.

After checking the Menu, I realized that the price depended on the kind of drink we ordered. Only American coffee or Coca-Cola cost NT$150. Without a second thought, I ordered a cup of American coffee and swiftly went to take some cookies and cakes. Sweet cakes were not my favorite, but I preferred those delicious and not too sweet cookies. Besides, there was sweet red and green beans soup, corn soup, fried rice and Italian noodles. “We can have a lot of noodles and rice for our dinner.” Both my friend and I got the same brilliant idea. Walking out from that restaurant with our stomachs stuffed, we both felt satisfied and thankful to the friend for sending the review to me.

I totally agreed with the positive review of In House, but only one drink was not enough as those cakes and cookies made me thirsty. To overcome this problem, it was better to bring our own water as they did not serve any.

I would recommend it to my friends and definitely like to go there again as the price was reasonable and the location was really convenient.

The spacious restaurant provides comfort and brightness.

Unlimited cakes and cookies to go with one drink
