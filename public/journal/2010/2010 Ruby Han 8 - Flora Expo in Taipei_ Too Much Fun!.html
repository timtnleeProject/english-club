Dec. 7, 2010

Flora Expo in Taipei: Too Much Fun!

This was the fourth time for me to visit the Flora Expo in Taipei. For the first time, I went to Da-jia Park out of mere curiosity. Unexpectedly, it was so much fun that I kept going back to enjoy it.

At 3 p.m. the two giant caterpillars made their way along the park street slowly cheered by a loud and exciting large crowd. Accompanying them were three automatic, cute dinosaurs and a girl disguised as a beautiful butterfly flapping her colorful wings (Picture 1). In order to let their children see the interesting parade clearly, many parents had their children ride on their shoulders. Suddenly, during a little disturbance, a man disguised as a giant gorilla jumped out of the procession and caught a little boy. His father quickly took the picture of his son held happily in the gorilla’s strong arms. When the parade gradually disappeared from sight, a lot of people remained sitting along both sides of the road, waiting for it to return. What is even more impressive is the portrait of Mona Lisa hanging on the wall of the Active Giant Egg. To everyone’s surprise, Mona Lisa suddenly blinked her eyes, moved down her eyebrows to frown, pushed out her lips to pout, and even opened her mouth wide to bare two of her teeth. Everyone admired the genius computer engineers for their humor and creativity. (Picture 2)

While we took a seat for some snacks, we saw five flower fairies in colorful tight suits and long feathers gracefully riding around the park and even climbing up the tall bamboo frame. I was so lucky that one of them consented to let me take a picture with him (Picture 3).

The Da-jia Park exhibited different kinds of Taiwan vegetables and fruits. Lots of parents took advantage of this good opportunity to teach their children about those plants that are hard to tell apart, such as onion and green onion, lemon trees and guava trees.

The visit of the Park was not completed until one rode the river boat to cross the river. Although it was so cold outside, everyone was excited to take the boat in line at 6:30 p.m. There were many white lilies floating on the water around the dock. Finally, our boat began moving ahead slowly. On one side of the river appeared flocks of geese flying elegantly, and on the other, many people were riding bikes in line. In the park, a lot of people were running around or walking with their dogs. This is a really wonderful feast on the sense of sight.

Though we spent a whole day there, we had just seen a small part of the rich and colorful Flora-Expo. On the way home, I heard a young woman from Hsin-chuang (新莊) say, “The Flora Expo has succeeded in introducing Taiwan to the whole world.”
