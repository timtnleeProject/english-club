Water-Frost Accommodation

My husband and I got lost among the thousands of options for package tours as we enjoyed the Travel Exhibition at the World Trade Center. The bottom line we agreed upon was that if we were to take a two-day trip, it must be a less-than-2-hour drive and demand a low budget. After careful consideration, we finally decided to go to the Water-Frost Accommodation in I-lan near the Tai-dung exercise park, not far away from Taipei. The trip would be manageably short, and we got a fairly low price of NT$2,990 per person to stay overnight in a special house surrounded by water with lots of bubbles of calcium carbonate and beautiful fish in it. Besides, we even got free ticks for both dinner and breakfast. (Picture 1)

By way of the Snow Tunnel, it took us only one hour and a half to arrive at the destinationâ€”the Water-Frost Accommodation. From a far distance, we could see some colorful houses standing on the water. It was so surprising that inside each room, part of the floor was made of glass through which we could see the water and some water plants floating to and fro. After we rested for a while, we rode a bicycle along the narrow, neat and seemingly endless trail with green fields on both sides. The open space, fresh air, and the glorious sunset impressed me deeply. I determined that I would be back again someday. (Picture 2)

When we were back from the ride, what was waiting for us was the prepared delicious barbecue for dinner. We sat beside the swimming pool and enjoyed the tasty food, the refreshing beer, and the romantic candle light. Because the water was so cold, no guests dared to swim there at night.

The next morning, after we enjoyed the sumptuous breakfast, the weather was getting warmer and warmer. The sounds of children laughing and water splashing were everywhere. It was such a pity that my husband swam only for two rounds before he got out of the water because the water was too cold. To my surprise, when we got back to our room, we found some fish swimming into and out of our house gracefully. I really appreciated this clever device because it provided us with interesting and delightful scenery. (Picture 3)

As we checked out at the counter and asked for a future preferential treatment, the manager told us that we would have to wait until the next Travel Exhibition at the World Trade Center.
