Nov. 2, 2010

Expo 2010 in Shanghai

Before I attended the Expo 2010 in Shanghai, one of my friends had warned me to be fully prepared for the enormous crowd and the long waiting lines, and so I brought with me a cute mini stool (Picture 1) which could be folded in half.

On Sep. 23, 2010, we arrived at Exposition Park. We saw with our own eyes that Entrance one’s plaza was crowded with thousands of people (Picture 2). Fortunately, because the long and winding line kept moving, about one hour later, we got in. It was a pity, however, that I never had the chance to use my cute stool. If you are a senior above the age of 75, you could pass the Green Tunnel without lining up.

Before entering the Expo Park, everyone needed to stand in line to wait for a security check. We could not bring any bottles of liquid with us. If you brought a liquid medicine, you must drink a little in front of those officials. It was done as strictly as in the airport. No knives were allowed in the park, so unfortunately, one of my smallest-sized Swiss Knives was found by metal detector, and I was forced to dump it into the trash can.

As our tour guide suggested, the Taiwan pavilion would return to Taiwan after the show, and the China exhibition would stay on the mainland forever, and so we decided we should visit other pavilions that had fewer visitors. The exhibit of Turkey was the first one we visited. Turks are the descendants of the ancient Ottoman Empire in the 12th and 13th centuries. They were proud of their old but glorious history and exhibited many historical relics like simple ceramics and textile around 7000 years ago. Similarly, the Irishmen boasted their ancient tomb-group 6000 years ago and the cathedral building constructed in 1200 B.C. From the sample wall of a house made of many thick stones, we saw how Irishmen earned a living—they raised poultry and made wool clothes by hand. When the cold winter came, all the family would sit beside the fireplace and drink a cup of hot coffee together (Picture 3).

We could not skip the Luxembourg pavilion, because Luxembourgers brought their precious and beautiful national treasure—the Golden color woman statue—and erected her highly in front of their exhibition hall (Picture 4). It is worthwhile to visit this kingdom of garden and international financial center.

Italy will be the host of Expo 2015. We had already heard about the different and magnificent Showrooms of Italy. Just as expected, we waited in line for an incredibly long time before we finally got into the grand and glorious building. A symphony including podium, cellos, harp, piano, drums and other instruments were hung on the tall wall of the first floor (Picture 5). On another wall were hung 12 pairs of models wearing different fancy clothes (Picture 6). In the center of the hall stood a purple big high-heeled shoe, on which were stuck over 100 regular high-heeled shoes (Picture 7). I thought they were telling people that Italy has been leading the world for centuries in literature, arts, and fashion. A variety of noodles on the huge counter of the second floor reminded people that Italian cuisine is one of the world’s best.
