Nov. 12, 2010

Korean Food

I felt happy to visit Korea as I liked spicy food very much, but being a vegetarian, I did not have many choices; when I delivered the note written in Korean, “I would like to order noodle or rice without meat or seafood,” I could only get sushi or fried-noodle most of the time. Daboki (炒年糕) was my favorite snack and it was sold everywhere especially after evening. Korean food might not arouse my interest, but the way Koreans enjoyed food did catch my attention.

In a famous shopping area, I saw scores of people surrounding a food vendor and holding a long stick with guts on it. They dipped the guts in a plate of sauce and then put them into their mouths. Taking a closer look, I surprisingly found that they shared the same plate of sauce. “Don’t they care about hygiene?” “What a strange eating custom!” I screamed in my mind.

Another interesting discovery was a restaurant which was just next to the hotel I stayed at. It was the only restaurant where customers were standing while roasting and tasting beef. The beef was not cheap, about NT$500 for two people, but customers still swarmed in every day. To satisfy my curiosity, I went to take a picture but left in no time as it was too smoky and crowded for me to breathe.

By accident, I found Loving Hut, a vegetarian chain restaurant. I was extremely relieved while having food there, for I finally got options other than Sushi and fried-noodle, such as a bowl of hot soup. What a blessing to have it in the cool autumn!
