Apr. 17, 2009

A Red Flower

“A group of earnest women with sincere hearts try to realize a dream together—a loving world free of cancer.” This was the conclusion of my team members after the Mar.17-19 training, held by Taiwan Breast Cancer Alliance (TBCA). There were eight teams with 60 participants from 33 breast cancer groups. Compared with the last two training sessions I had joined, this one had a more flexible schedule, but it was more complete.

Instead of having classes right after we arrived, we had a relaxing lunch and a 1-hour nap before our first class. In the first class, we set some rules for ourselves. The one rule that impressed me much was: if someone was late for the class, we would call her name and say to her loudly, “It’s good to have you.” It seemed like a chiding, but sounded like a compliment. During the 3-day training, whoever on behalf of her team went on the stage to share her observation for each class would get positive feedback. Everyone would point out the excellence of her sharing as the teacher told us, “When you magnify others’ advantages, you won’t see their shortages.” I had never heard of such a saying but I liked it because I thought it would help me get along well with others.

All the knowledge we learned was to help us find the goal for our patient groups as well as the goal of TBCA. At evening classes, we rearranged eight teams into four teams to set up a goal. After two nights’ discussion, in the afternoon of the third day, we presented our goal by performing a short play. To my great surprise, three of four teams had the same goal—raising the checking rate of breast cancer to increase the cure rate so as to have a loving world free of cancer.

At the end of the training, the teacher shared with us a song called “A Red Flower.” He said that we were just like the red flower. Without soil, it still bloomed so well on the stone. He continued with a very soft voice, “You as a group of cancer patients simply want to remind people to check their breasts regularly as you can’t bear to see anyone suffer from cancer treatments like you.” Few listened to the sweet song without shedding tears. From the teacher’s sharing, I clearly knew that the power of the 60 women was unlimited, that they could make something different if they followed their goals. Feeling proud, I was glad to be one of them.

Three volunteers from Wang-Fang hospital patient group attended this training.

All of us wish to light up others’ lives through our love.
