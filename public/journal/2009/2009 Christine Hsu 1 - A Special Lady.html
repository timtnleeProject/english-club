Mar. 27, 200

A Special Lady

One week after Chinese Lunar New Year, though I still soaked myself in the joyful holiday atmosphere, I had no choice but to go to Chang-Gung Memorial Hospital, Linkou Branch, to fulfill my duty as a volunteer. It had been a dull day until I met the special lady.

“Excuse me, may I ask you a question?” A well-dressed lady politely interrupted me when I was talking with some cleft-lipped children’s parents at the free clinic. After a small talk with her, I was surprised to find that the 8-year-old girl she brought along was not her daughter.

Seven years ago, she accompanied her sister’s daughter here for a cleft-lip surgery. Their roommate was a one-year-old girl from Suao, I-lan County. The girl’s mother ran away from home right after giving birth to her. Moreover, her father was over 65 and the only one who took care of her during daytime was her 70-year-old aunt. Being illiterate, however, the old aunt felt rather helpless whenever she took the girl to the hospital for regular checks. The two sisters felt for their difficult situation and decided to give them a hand. In the past seven years, every time when the aunt took the girl to Taipei to see the doctor, the lady or her sister would take turns to go to the hospital to offer whatever help, including driving them to the train station.

I felt so lucky to meet a lady with such a beautiful mind, and I did admire her perseverance, which kept her doing such a selfless deed for seven years. I still remembered how troublesome it was to bring my cleft-lip daughter to have regular checks. The lady’s compassion and selflessness totally overwhelmed me. It is amazing that so many people are doing good deeds in their own way in every corner around the world.

On the first Tuesday of each month, I went to the Linkou Branch of Chang Gung Memorial Hospital. There, I shared my experiences with parents with cleft-lip children and answered their questions.
