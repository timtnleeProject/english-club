May 19, 2011

We Can Do More

When I was young, I always asked myself, “Why is life meaningful?” just like the main character in the novel of Qong Yao, especially when I was struggling in difficulties. After undergoing various experiences in life, I found that I would have a wonderful time if I didn’t just think about myself all the time but actually did something for others. For example, I felt happy after I made a phone call to a bus company to show my appreciation of some kind and polite bus driver. If a very sick family member got well under my great care, I felt important and valuable.

Muzha Park is a very small area with dense trees. It can be dangerous at night if it was all dark. I called the secretary of the Wenshan District Administration Center about it. He told me he would need time before he could give me an answer. A few days later, he promised me that those lights in the park would be turned on at 7 p.m. every day.

I was satisfied with almost everything about the Runtai Jingdian Mansion, where I had lived for years, except perhaps the inconvenience caused by the drying racks at the rooftop of the building. You couldn’t imagine how tall the two drying racks were. Well, they were 163 cm tall, so tall that I needed to stand on my tiptoes to hang my laundry including clothes, sheets and quilts (Picture 1). I proposed at the resident conference that we either put two bricks under the racks or replace them with shorter ones. Having got no responses for nearly a year, I proposed it again at the next meeting. Unexpectedly, a few months later, I found the committee not only put four bricks under the two old racks but also got a new one, a shorter one (Picture 2).

Pedestrians on the third section of Muzha Road always crossed the street directly because they found it troublesome to walk another 50 meters to use the zebra crossing or traffic lights. I figured it was hazardous, so I called the hotline 1999 to demand the city government to mark the crosswalk between Alley 49 and the Muzha bus station. Ten days later, they sent me a letter of disapproval with a seemingly sensible but actually incorrect reason (Picture 3). I talked about it to the community head, an assemblyman, and even one of my English Club members Rita. After some time, the zebra crossing was finally painted out, which made me very happy.

Dr. Sun Yet-sen said, “The wiser serve a great number of people, when the less intelligent serve a few.” Although we are just average, ordinary people, we could do more for the society, for people around us. The more we do, the better we feel about ourselves and the world we live in.
