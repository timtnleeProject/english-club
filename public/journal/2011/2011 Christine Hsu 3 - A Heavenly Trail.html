May 20, 2011

A Heavenly Trail

During the noon time of weekdays, if I am not busy working at the office, I will take a walk at the CKS Memorial Hall. Since last November, I have been taking a stroll after lunch regularly. It takes me around six minutes to walk from my office to the memorial hall, one of the best places for taking a walk.

To get rid of my belly fat, I decided that every day I should take a walk during noon break besides swimming in the early morning. It also helped me to get fresh air after three hours’ work. It only took me about 30 minutes to walk around the memorial hall in one circle and the various picturesque scenes made me forget my tiredness. In winter, there would be plum blossoms and cherry blossoms. The red cherry blossom especially got visitors’ full attention and made them linger on for long.

The place in the park I liked to stay best was near the MRT station. Two speakers were put on the tree to play classic music. When the weather was not so cold or hot, I would lie on the wooden chair and take a nap. Lying under a giant tree and listening to the beautiful music, I always fell asleep easily. How lucky am I to be able to enjoy such a wonderful place without having to pay a cent. Most people come here for jogging or walking while some come to play Chinese chess. There was a great outdoor chess area on one side of Ai-Guo E. Road. There were some wooden desks with carved chess boards and wooden chairs with sun umbrellas to protect players from the sunburn. The only thing you needed to bring was your chess. How thoughtful! Though I did not play chess, I would love to have a picnic with friends there for we could admire those plants around us when we enjoyed the food.

The summer heat during noon time would kill me, but thanks to the corridors, I still could take a walk there or view the calligraphy show or painting exhibition. The exhibition room was the greatest place for me to take a rest. All in all, I did enjoy the noon break walking in such a splendid park.
