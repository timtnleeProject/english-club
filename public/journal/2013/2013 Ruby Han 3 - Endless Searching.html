Oct. 10, 2013

Endless Searching

For the reunion party of my thirtieth graduation anniversary, one of my students Wang Yu-lin tried to Google me and surprisingly found a couplet of calligraphy I had written posted in a blog by a so-called “Taiwanese Grandma” in Switzerland.

“Switzerland is,” Wang later told me, “the foreign country I’m most familiar with besides the States. My husband used to work there for two and a half years, and I used to enjoy my summer and winter there. Almost as if in a trance, once I recognized the stylish and elegant calligraphy writing of Teacher Han’s, I took it for granted that ‘Taiwanese Grandma’ was Teacher Han, and that Teacher Han was living in Switzerland. It didn’t take long for the Switzerland-based Taiwanese Grandma to answer the message I sent her. She told me she was not who I think she was but offered to check the contact information of Teacher Han for me. What a beautiful mistake I’ve made. I couldn’t help but feel amazed.”

The couplet of calligraphy writing— which reads “A constant heart is the moon that does not wax and wane; a freed mind is the cloud that floats and swirls” (一心似月無盈缺, 萬事如雲任舒捲)—inspired Wang and other students of her class to invite me to write in calligraphy the theme phrase “Hundred-Year Green Destiny” (百年綠緣) to be hung in the versions to make sure they are neither monotonous nor illegible.

It was a beautiful encounter three decades ago when teacher and students met and spent three precious years together, understanding, caring about and loving each other. We were all very grateful to the organizers of this grand event--Taipei First Girl Senior High School’s students and Alumni Association. Thanks to their endless searching, the grass of the green garden gathered from home or abroad to have the green destiny continued.
