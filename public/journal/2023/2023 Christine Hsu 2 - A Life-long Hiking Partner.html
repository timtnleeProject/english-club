A Life-long Hiking Partner

Because of COVID-19, the government closed all the civic sports centers, including the swimming pools, in late 2021. During weekdays, the only exercise I could do was running, but I could hardly breathe while doing it with a mask on. As I got more time on weekends, I began to climb mountains near my house. The more I hiked, the more I felt I needed to seek greater challenges. Then I figured that the only way to explore more trails was to join hiking groups.

Surfing on the Internet for a few days, I decided to join a group named Du-Du to climb mountains in Xindian on October 31, 2021. When I arrived at the Xindian MRT station, I was surprised to find no hikers there. I quickly calmed myself down and walked to the bus stop, in a hope to catch up with the group at Wulai terminal station, the designated starting point for hiking. I lined up behind five hikers at the bus stop and found that one of them was looking at me curiously. Little did I know that the man, Mr. X, would play such an important role in my future mountain climbing activities. After some warm greetings, Mr. X invited me to join them. I was not sure if I could actually meet the first hiking group, so I thought Iâ€™d better join the second group instead.

After that first amazing hiking experience, we have been hiking together for the past two years. Besides me, Mr. X also invited four or five friends to hike together. From their conversations, I got to know that they each owned a laundry shop and worked with their wives. I was unfamiliar with the laundry business as I always did my own laundry. It was surprising to know that they worked from 8:30 to 21:30 and only had Sunday off. I felt lucky that I did not marry a laundry shopkeeper though most of their wives must be leading a luxurious life. Besides the long working hours, they also had little time to make new friends, so they all cherished their friends that shared the same hobby of mountain climbing.

Mr. X revealed to me his passion for mountain climbing, which helped him release his stress, comfort his mind, and stretch his muscles after he was confined in the store doing laundry for six consecutive days. He also invited me to be his partner for climbing as many mountains as possible for the rest of our lives. He said over the past 10 years he had never met a woman with as much energy, fully capable of keeping up with his steps and never quit mountain climbing due to bad weather. I accepted his invitation without hesitation since I always enjoyed the warm atmosphere created by him and his friends during hiking. Besides, what can be more relieving to have such a responsible and considerate friend as a companion for mountaineering?.

In addition to regular hiking, about every six months Mr. X would invite about 30 friends to hike on an easier trail and enjoy a late-lunch gathering.

This picture was taken by my hiking partner at Taipei Feitsui Reservoir.

He asked me to host this mid-year event with him and help do some paperwork. Making invitation posters or reserving a restaurant was not a big deal to me, so I accepted it immediately. In order to save time for collecting registers' information, we asked participants to fill in a Google form instead of leaving a message via communication app Line. The disadvantage was that I had to spend a lot of time teaching them about using the Google form, but the advantage was that we could check the registration status online at any time. I believed our efforts would pay off next time.

During our planning of this event, I discovered that Mr. X was very different from my friends who were of his age. He felt amazed at everything I had done for this event, but it was really nothing special to me. As he has never had to use a computer at work ever since he joined the business at the age of 18, he had little understanding of 3C equipment. I had never seen a person like him, and because of him I got to know that not everyone under 60 was good at operating a computer or cell phone. Interestingly, I rather appreciate the chance for us to learn from each other and grow together. For example, two years ago, we both knew nothing about an off-line map app, but now, not only could we use it to avoid getting lost, but we also learned more hiking tips to keep ourselves safe on the mountain.

Truth be told, meeting like-minded people on the mountain might be more difficult than winning a lottery, so I thought it must have been Godâ€™s best arrangement for me. With the rich hiking experiences of Mr. X, I know I will be safely exploring more wonderful peaks and enjoying more magnificent sceneries.
