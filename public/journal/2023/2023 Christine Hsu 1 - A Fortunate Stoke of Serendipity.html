A Fortunate Stroke of Serendipity

On December 8, 2022, I found myself at the ICU in a diaper and patient clothes. There were some machines beside my ward to check my vital signs. I tried hard to search my memory but got no clues as to what had happened until a nurse told me that I got in a biking accident, causing my brain to bleed. â€œNo way! I am an excellent biker,â€ I yelled at the nurse. But the truth was I fell down from my bike for an unknown reason after crossing an intersection.

Fortunately I only stayed at the ICU for one day; or else, I would have gone crazy since I could not get off the bed, which means I had to do everything on the bed. After being transferred to the general ward, it was still difficult for me to get to the toilet with two machines connected to me, but this was so much better than being imprisoned at ICU. I quickly got well and was discharged after eight-day hospitalization.

Right after I went back home, I rushed to the police office to view the monitor video from the video surveillance system in order to find out my life savers. I saw myself riding on the sidewalk to the MRT Station. After passing Wanfang Road and slipping to Mucha Road, the bike suddenly stopped. Then I fell down on the sidewalk. Immediately, a young man came and sheltered my head from the rain with an umbrella. Next, a postman stopped his motorcycle and walked toward me to do something, which could not be seen clearly as I was lying down under a big tree. At the same time, a driver also got out of his car to help me. As I watched part of the video where passersby joined in one after another to help a stranger, my eyes streamed and streaked. I wondered if I could ever repay their kindness, so I decided to make donations to charity and vowed to be a lifelong vegetarian. However, I did not think that was enough.

Twenty-eight years ago, because of my elder daughter, I began to do clinic volunteer work at Chang Gung Memorial Hospital. After over a decade, however, I turned to be a network volunteer for some reason. Undergoing this recent accident, I strongly believed that it was a call for me to get back to clinic volunteer work to help more people for the rest of my life.
