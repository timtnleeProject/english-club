June 11, 2009

Special Events

One of my friends told me that she had seen a lot of meteors falling into the Pacific Ocean at the beach in Hualien County. From then on, any information on TV or newspapers about meteor showers would get my attention immediately.

Once we got to the Zheng Da campus next to the dike at night lay on the meadow waiting for the meteor to come. To my disappointment, we only saw a few meteors. Some people said if we wanted to see the meteor, we should stay in a darker place to get a good view.

Not long after, the media told us that the biggest meteor shower of this century would be coming. All people in Taiwan would be able to see the magnificent scenery if we watched in a place where there was no light pollution.

Before it was coming, many people went at night to the darkest places like the beach of Kengtung or Mt. Ali, or Mt. Seven Stars above the Yangming Mountains. Bringing some mattresses and clothes, my husband, my daughter, my sister and I went to the Beizhen Junior High School’s playground. Wearing thick clothes, we lay on our backs on the mattresses and then looked at the dark sky attentively.

Around 11 pm, the first shooting star pierced through the sky and rushed onto the earth. And another one followed and another one and another one… We screamed loudly, constantly. Unexpectedly, the screams from the Zheng Da campus broke out at the same time from within the wall. Up to now, I never forgot that wonderful night of watching numerous shooting stars rushing through the sky and hearing loud exclamations from all over places. Our voice all became husky because of shouting—we were just too excited and happy.
