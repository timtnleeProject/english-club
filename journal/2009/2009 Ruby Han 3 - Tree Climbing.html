June 4, 2009

Tree Climbing

From the poster inside the Cheng Da campus, I learned about a tree climbing activity to be held in front of Da Yong on Monday morning, 10-12 am. On May 11, I asked for one hour’s leave from my volunteer work and rushed to the place excitedly to see the tree climbing show. To my big surprise, nothing was going on at the pointed place and no one knew about this activity. Finally someone told me to check with the Cheng Chih University’s General Affairs Department. As I was informed, the tree they had planned for people to climb was one of the important preserves meant to be protected, and so they later decided to climb instead the tree in the backyard of the school principal’s house, which took me five minutes to walk there.

I saw, as I arrived, many students under the big tree, happily sharing with each other the food and drinks they brought along. As many as ten rope equipment had been hung on the strong enough branches. Some students had had a seat in the air (about four stories high) casually and elegantly and said hello proudly to everyone passing by. One of them claimed that he had a wonderful dream on the top of the tree. And another said he got a terrific view and could see the school’s Cultural Center in the distance.

Of course, some students just practiced climbing hard. The coach who had a license of international tree climbing taught them how to knit the rope, how to put the foot in the loop, and how to climb correctly and patiently.

While I was watching the novel activity, a young man invited me to climb the tree. NO WAY! I told him, “Please don’t trick me. I’m just here to see your great show and take some pictures of all of you young guys.”

“Wow, what’s it on my head?” a student in the air shouted loudly. He got down to the ground as quickly as possible. The interesting extracurricular activity ended with great fun.
