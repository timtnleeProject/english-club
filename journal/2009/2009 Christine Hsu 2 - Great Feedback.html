Apr. 3, 2009

Great Feedback

It was the fourth time I met Branka, a Canadian bone marrow courier. On March 17, when she saw me at the International Airport, she cuddled me tightly and said, “I have been so excited since I knew I would come to Taiwan again!”

For job’s shake, at least twice per month, Branka had to go overseas to take matched bone marrow from other hospitals for her hospital’s patients, but she never got to experience our warm pick-up service. When she first arrived in Taiwan three years ago, she was surprised to see a volunteer of Tzu Chi Foundation waiting for her at the arrival hall, and on the way to the hotel, the volunteer comforted her with warm words and sincere care. Moreover, on the next day to Hualien, another volunteer sent her to the domestic airport. When she was back to Taipei, I was waiting to drive her to the International Airport. In my car, Branka told me that one of her friends Laurie had come here a year before, and told her how hospitable Tzu Chi volunteers were. Now she finally experienced it by herself and the feeling was so good as she never received such great care in other countries.

Branka was not only impressed by our pick-up service, but also grateful to our volunteers in Vancouver, who regularly visited a folks’ home where her grandmother stayed before passing away. Though she never met Tzu Chi volunteers there, from her grandmother’s satisfied smile, she could tell how well those volunteers took care of her grandmother. Branka straightened her thumb and said to me, “What you guys do is really great!”

On March 20, it was time for her to go back to Canada. I picked her up from Taipei Main Station after she got the bone marrow from Hualien. We went to visit Tzu Chi General Hospital, Taipei Branch. Before we stepped into the hospital, Branka’s sight was attracted by those balconies as she never saw anything like it at any hospitals in Canada. After our introduction, she realized that the balcony not only protected the ward from strong sunlight to save electric power, but also offered a safe working place for window-washers. While Branka lightly touched the smooth surface of the pillars, she deeply felt how thoughtful we were. The complete equipment and warm atmosphere of Palliative Medical Ward surprised her even more. She asked us to take pictures of the wooden floor, paintings on the wall, the corridors, the bathrooms, and many other details so that she could share with her colleagues. She was astonished that we also offered patients’ family oil massage therapy as well as different kinds of praying rooms. “Can non-Buddhist patients come to this hospital?” The nurse and I replied together, “Of course. Actually, some of our doctors are Christians or Catholics.”

During the past three years, Branka had come here to take bone marrow three times. She told me that the three patients recovered very well and one of them became a spokesman of bone marrow transplantation, encouraging patients who were waiting for matched bone marrow or who were under transplantation. She felt it incredible that each case from Taiwan turned out successful.

Being one member of the bone marrow courier pick-up team of Tzu Chi Foundation, I am greatly inspired by Branka’s positive feedback. Pick-up task is not easy since it takes not only time but also money (for petrol and toll fee, for example). But it is truly worth it since such contributions have warmed the bone marrow couriers’ hearts and engraved an unforgettable memory in their mind.
