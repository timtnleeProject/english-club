June 5, 2008

Cotton Clothes

In the winter nights, the fresh-smelling, warm and soft quilt always reminds me of my mother. While the time of the season changes to welcome shiny days, my mother would take the quilt and heavy clothes outdoors and expose them under the sun to eliminate their moldiness.

My mother was a self-disciplined and well-organized person, and she was also talented in embroidery, knitting and weaving clothes though never having received any related lessons. Many people were amazed when they saw our pillows embroidered with extraordinarily beautiful pictures of flowers or birds.

Besides doing chores to make our home comfortable, my mom devoted herself to doing side jobs to help her children complete schooling. Because my mother never gambled—gambling was the only important entertainment in the old days besides gossiping, she won the trust of many neighbors, who put their house keys at my home when they went out for business. Sometimes there were up to 10 different keys on the walls of my house.

As an honest person, my mom couldn’t say any honeyed words to friends or neighbors. My father always blamed her for being too straightforward, which, he was afraid, may have hurt people’s feelings. However, my father appreciated her as the best mother and wife ever and praised her often behind her back.

While I gave birth to my son, my mother came to Taipei and took care of us. She brought along a lot of things she made by herself, including a precious piece of cotton baby clothes with a design of a little heart on its collar. Therefore, my baby son enjoyed his first warm and comfortable winter. The picture with my mom holding my son in the cotton clothes was one of my most cherished photos.

Time flies, and my son has become a father of a daughter. At the end of last year, he brought his wife and daughter home from abroad for the Chinese Lunar New Year. One day, I had my granddaughter put on the cotton clothes once worn by her father as a baby, and I took a treasurable picture of the father and daughter. It seemed as if my kind, great mother were standing just in front of us and blessing us all the time.
