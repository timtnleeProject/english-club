Dec. 3, 2010

Sky Garden

“The garden on your roof top is pretty! Do you see it?” A neighbor told me when we met at the elevator. “Is it the one I applied for half a year ago?” I thought to myself and replied, “Oh, they have already built it up, I will go check it later.” I could not wait to tell my family the good news because we’ve anticipated the wonderful sky garden.

Back to last April, for establishing a garden city, the government set up a program to beautify 100 communities by offering plants. After a meeting, all commissioners of my community agreed to apply. At that time, I registered for my balcony and rooftop.

After a few months, my community guard gave me a bag of mud and plants with a small rectangular pot. He told me that kind of plant would produce white flowers. We watered it every day, but never saw a flower bloom.

I completely forgot about the plants for my roof top before my community guard asked me to sign the construction confirmation of a sky garden. “Don’t they just give me some plants to decorate my rooftop?” I asked the guard. He led me to the roof top and showed me the area they would build the garden, but I still had no pictures in mind until I saw it last week.

The garden covered about 60 percent of the space of my rooftop. There were about five kinds of plants and two kinds of them would bloom in spring and summer. According to the gardeners, my house’s indoor temperature would drop around four degrees in summer. My family was looking forward to a garden to relieve our suffering from the scorching summer heat. Last summer, we watered the rooftop right after arriving home almost every evening; from now on, we did not need to do it anymore because the automatic sprinklers not only watered the plants but also our roof top.

The beautiful sky garden cheered up my family.

The automatic sprinkler not only watered the plants but also our roof top, how greatful!
